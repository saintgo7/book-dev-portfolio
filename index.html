Picking the Right Projects

Quality beats quantity. Three polished projects with thoughtful documentation will land you more interviews than a graveyard of half-finished repos.

When evaluating whether a project belongs in your portfolio, ask:

  • Does it demonstrate a skill that is relevant to the roles I am targeting?
  • Can I articulate the problem it solves in one sentence?
  • Would I be comfortable discussing every line of code in an interview?
  • Is there a live demo or a compelling screenshot/gif I can show?

Avoid tutorial clones unless you have significantly extended the original. A to-do app is fine if you added real-time sync, end-to-end encryption, and a custom offline mode. A to-do app that stops at the tutorial checkpoint is not.

ðŸ’¡ Tip: Open-source contributions count. A well-scoped PR to a popular repository signals that you can work in an unfamiliar codebase â€” exactly the skill every team needs.

SEO & Discoverability

You do not need to become an SEO expert, but a few straightforward practices will get your portfolio indexed and surfaced when someone Googles your name or your niche.

  • Use a <title> tag that includes your name and role
  • Add <meta name="description"> to every page
  • Include an og:image so links to your site look great when shared
  • Submit your site to Google Search Console after launch
  • Write at least one blog post or case study with a long-tail keyword in the title
